Sagi Gilad

Founder & CEO

linkedin.com/in/sagi-gilad

I started my career in education.

Ten years working with students — four of them in Higher Education — showed me something most systems still struggle to acknowledge: the student mental health crisis is not only a clinical challenge. It’s an institutional infrastructure problem.

Universities face an impossible equation: demand for support is rising rapidly while clinical capacity, budgets, and liability constraints remain fixed. At the same time, students are already turning to AI for support — often outside institutional oversight.

Over the past year, conversations with university presidents, counseling directors, CFOs, and general counsels across the U.S. made one thing clear: the problem isn’t a lack of tools. It’s the absence of governance infrastructure.

That realization led us to build HEIAxis — a governance-grade control plane for campus care.

Our mission is to help institutions transform fragmented mental health ecosystems into systems that are auditable, accountable, scalable, and adaptive to AI-native student behavior.

Because the next generation of campus care won’t be defined by more apps. It will be defined by the infrastructure that makes care safe, accountable, and scalable.

Your institution is already carrying the responsibility.
It’s time to govern it with the architecture it deserves.
