The Governance Gap

$55 billion in annual spend. No control layer.

Student Affairs carries enormous institutional responsibility — and operates on systems designed for fragments, not flows. EHRs document. Case tools isolate. Portals report activity, not execution. No single system routes, owns, escalates, audits, and governs across the full student journey. The result is institutional liability, compliance exposure, budget waste, and no defensible record of care.

The Breakpoint

Three forces converged. The control-layer moment is now.

01

AI Without Governance

AI is entering campus workflows before institutional standards exist. Every uncontrolled tool is a compliance and data sovereignty risk. The institution needs a governed layer before — not after — deployment.

02

Vendor Sprawl by Default

Institutions have accumulated siloed vendors with no unified governance, no consolidated evidence, and no performance accountability. Each new contract deepens the fragmentation.

CFOs and Boards demand auditable ROI from Student Affairs spend. Activity metrics are no longer sufficient. Institutions need defensible outcome evidence — before the next budget review.
